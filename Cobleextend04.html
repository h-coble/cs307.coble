RGB values are numbers to represent how much red, blue, and green are mixed to result in the color specified on a scale from 1 - 255. Hex values do the same, but in hexadecimal from 00 - FF for each color. HSL is designed for human readability. The values represent Hue, Saturation, and Lightness. Hue is a degree measurement on the color wheel, and the other two are percentages. Saturation is the % of how "colorful" the color is: lower percentages are more gray and higher percentages are more vibrant. Lightness is straightforward: how bright is the color?

Preferred color code format: I personally prefer Hex because I have had the most experience with it. I find it easier to use than HSL because it's just color mixing instead of having to worry about "shading" as I would describe it. I find the hex values easier to think about and write than rgb values due to fewer keystrokes.

HWB summary:
HWB stands for Hue, whiteness, and blackness and was created with the idea that a user can specify a hue then mix black and white into it to get the desired color. Black and white values are percentages, and hue is a degree measurement of the color wheel.

CMYK summary:
CMYK stands for cyan magenta yellow and key (black). It's a subtractive color model; it's intended to be used with a light/white background and the colors subtract color from the white, resulting in a desired shade. This contrasts from the RGB model which is additive. In RGB, full values added together results in white and 0 is black. In the CMYK model, the inverse is true.
